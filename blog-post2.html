SUCCESS DETERMINANTS

posted on August 13, 2018

A person cannot succeed outwardly beyond the level he has succeeded inwardly. One’s external reality is commensurate to his internal reality. Success can be defined as the realization of predetermined goals

There are two kinds of success (Josh1:8) - Good and Bad Success

Good success is the progressive realization of God-intended goals. God’s priority is legislating Kingdom business on earth. Every success has a beginning/ point of commencement. Decisions create beginnings. A decision is a position, opinion or judgment passed or taken concerning certain things after considering available information. Every man is a walking warehouse of decisions.

SEVEN TRUTHS ABOUT DECISIONS

  1. Decisions are judgements, so decision-makers are judges.
  2. Decisions are based on available information. Thus, the quality of my decision is dependent on the quality of information available to me.
  3. Decisions are made between two or more options.
  4. Decisions are made to favour one’s value system.
  5. Every decision has an outcome. The outcome of a judgment is dependent on the type of judgement made.
  6. Every decision taken is a reflection of one’s principles.
  7. Every decision taken makes the decision-maker, thus a person is a sum total of his decisions.

Poor decisions produce poor outcomes, and in turn, build poor lives.

We must learn to make decisions to favour the following:

  1. Discovery Of Purpose- Purpose precedes creation, manufacturing, production and is more importance.(Prov 18:21) Until Purpose/Divine Will is discovered, life is lived experimentally. Purpose is an expression of God’s divine assignment. The greatest failure is succeeding in the wrong assignment.(Prov 11:14, 15:22, 20:18, 24:6) Purpose is a discovery, not a decision.
  2. Potential Development- Potentials are hidden ability, untapped energy, unused strength. The area of a person’s potential is the area of his strength. Purpose determines potentials but potentials fulfill purpose. The richest ornament in existence is not air or in the sea but in Man.(II Cor 4:7) Types of Potentials: a) Natural Potentials- innate/inherent ones. b) Acquired Potentials- learned or given. All men have the ability to acquire abilities. Potentials are God’s gifts to us, developed potentials are our gifts to God. Potentials are hidden, if achieved, they are no longer potentials. What one can accomplish, shows that there is a higher ground. Potentials are developed; undeveloped potentials are crude talents while developed potentials are refined skills. Skill is the intentional application of the mind on talents. Keys to unlocking potentials are the Right Words and Demand.
  3. Good Partnerships (I Cor 15:33)- Company accounts for fifty percent of human capital development. There are the inanimate and animate companies.(Ps 101:3) Partnerships determine perspectives; once there is association, there is learning. Accommodate everyone but regulate their access to your life.(Ps 1:1) Any decision made to disfavour God results in a shaky foundation. What is in a man’s mind is more important than what is in his hands, because it is what is in his mind that determines what enters his hands.
  4. Personal Development- This could be done through:
    • Reading- (I Tim 4:13) Read the writings of the Prophets (the Scriptures), the Professionals (Books) and the Press (Newspapers). However when listening to the press, remember to filter all you hear with the Word Of God.
    • Motivation- The growth of any organization, group or family is dependent on the growth of the individual members in it.(Acts 4:36-37) Personal development is essential because the quality of my relationships will not rise above the quality of my personal development.When God speaks to a man, He produces wings on which the man can soar.
